Introduction to Wonderland Time

Wonderland Time is a non-custodial cryptocurrency portfolio tracking and analytics platform that gives users a unified view of their crypto holdings and activities across various decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols and exchanges.

Launched in 2020, Wonderland Time has gained popularity amongst crypto users as one of the easiest ways to get an aggregated overview of a user's crypto wealth, staked assets, open protocols and more - without having to manually log into each individual service.

In this article, we will provide an introduction to Wonderland Time - exploring its key features, how it functions, the importance of portfolio tracking in DeFi, and the risks associated with using the platform.

Core Features of Wonderland Time

Some of the core features that users enjoy on Wonderland Time include:

  • Aggregated Portfolio View: Shows a combined overview of token balances, wallet addresses, total portfolio value across protocols.

  • Activity Analytics: Tracks historical deposits, withdrawals, transaction records, staking records, lending activities etc.

  • Protocol Connection: Sync portfolio information automatically by connecting to supported DeFi protocols like Compound, Aave, Curve via API/JavaScript.

  • Wallet Support: Supports popular wallets like MetaMask, Trust, Coinbase, WalletConnect for login and authentication.

  • Mobile App: Cross-platform mobile apps available on iOS and Android for access on the go.

  • Currency Conversion: Convert balances and values to desired fiat currencies for easy FIAT value tracking.

  • Risk Analytics: Scorecards highlight risk exposure across protocols based on TVL, volume, age and other factors.

  • Price Data: Integrated price feeds from sources like CoinGecko for accurate portfolio valuation.

  • Investment Performance: Tools to track token/portfolio value changes and returns over time.

This wide range of features is accessible through a simple yet powerful interface, giving users powerful tracking without manual legwork.

How it Works

Wonderland Time acts as a read-only portal that connects to users' crypto wallets and the underlying DeFi protocols they interact with:

  1. Users connect their wallet like MetaMask to Wonderland Time via wallet integration.

  2. Wonderland Time then retrieves wallet activity history from the blockchain like transfers, approvals, and interactions with protocols.

  3. It integrates with supported DeFi protocols' APIs to sync activities like deposits, borrows, yields from there.

  4. All this scattered data is then consolidated into a unified dashboard and analytics.

  5. No private keys or funds are held by Wonderland Time - it simply acts as an interface to view portfolio holdings and activities.

This permissionless, non-custodial model makes it a safe platform for users to gain insights without privacy or security risks.

Importance of Portfolio Tracking

With the rise of DeFi, tracking one's crypto wealth and activities has become increasingly complex as users interact with several platforms:

  • DeFi protocols typically don't offer portfolio-level views.

  • Manual logging into each protocol is time-consuming.

  • Critical to track performance, yields, exposures across networked finance.

  • Difficulties in tax reporting and compliance without centralized records.

  • Risks of forgotten funds or inactive protocols without tracking.

Aggregated portfolio tracking solutions like Wonderland Time have therefore become crucial for easier overviews, compliance, and ensuring users remain updated on their expanding investments in this burgeoning sector.

Risks of Using Wonderland Time

While Wonderland Time provides valuable services, some risks do exist:

  • API/syncing issues could lead to temporary data mismatches or delays.

  • Blockchain errors could impact syncing if transactions fail to record properly.

  • Reliant on supported protocols - won't track wallets/exchanges without APIs.

  • Users still need to monitor connected accounts/wallets for cyber threats.

  • Non-custodial means Wonderland Time isn't liable for funds if connected services are hacked.

  • Regulatory risks if user data is ever exposed or accessed without consent.

  • UX/feature overhauls could impact syncing of historical data ported over.

  • Centralized service - single point of failure if Wonderland Time has outages or gets hacked.

To minimize such risks, responsible data policies, robust architectures and proactive safety measures are important for any portfolio tracking service provider.
